Shopping for a Mortgage Online: Why Are Homebuyers Being Misled?

Shopping for a mortgage online is like going through a maze of mediocre websites and misinformation. The fact is that a lot of mortgage companies have gotten lazy: “Interest rates are at their lowest level! Let me ask you just a few questions and I’ll get you the lowest rate on the market!”

It’s easy to throw out low mortgage interest rates and APRs. We’re all attracted to the shiny objects.

Imagine a regular homebuyer shopping for a mortgage on Google and ending up on one of these mortgage referral sites. Most of these sites think, “Ok, he’s here. He’s gotta be looking for the lowest rate possible, so let’s feed it to him!”

Meanwhile, the person doesn’t really know what loan type and loan features he’s looking for. He simply knows, “I’ve heard about a lot of people getting into trouble with their mortgages and I want to make sure I get the best deal for my specific needs and profile.”

How effective is it to assume a borrower knows what he or she wants before shopping for a mortgage?

Which Mortgage Calculator Should I Use to Calculate My Monthly Mortgage Payments?

Choosing the right mortgage calculator is very important to correctly estimate your monthly mortgage payment. Unfortunately, most mortgage calculators available in search results are not very accurate and, to be blunt, pretty useless. Don’t believe me, just watch!…sorry Bruno Mars. Let’s look at an example.

Katie currently rents an apartment in the city for $1,200/month. She and her husband Mark want to buy a home to raise a family in and think they can afford a house in the $250,000 range.

They know that their monthly payment cannot be higher than $1,500. They also know that there is a high possibility that Mark could get transferred for work within the next five years. They’ve made a point of saving as much as possible for a down payment and figure they can afford to put down 5%, or $12,500.

  • Current Rent Payment: $1,200
  • Prospective Home Value: $250,000
  • Down Payment: $12,500
  • Maximum monthly payment they can afford: $1,500
  • Time expected to stay in the home: 5 years or less

How much can Katie and her husband expect as their total monthly mortgage payment?
